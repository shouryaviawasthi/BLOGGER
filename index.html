SAVE THE SOIL...

Soil is the habitat upon which is zillions of life thrive because of that thriving life, everyone grows. We eat food other animals grow everything happens because soil is . Once there is no richness in soil, then you have forsaken the planet in many ways . We talk of carbon, we talk of emissions, we talk of pollution at various levels . Before all that we need to address the most important concern, because there is a misunderstanding in the world . There is a campaign to project civic issues as environmental issues. Right now if plastic bags are floating around in the oceans this is not an ecological issue this is a civic issue. If our rivers are polluted this can be fixed in one or two years. If you are set up the necessary purification plants and enforce the law these are civic issues which can be fixed with a little bit of law enforcement and awareness, but the real issue is soil. The real problem is soil extension because in the last 30 years 80% of the biomass insects have been disappeared from the planet. So in 45 years time or 50 years time there will be serious food crisis on the planet . When food crisis come well whoever has the biggest guns will come and take the food and the chaos and the suffering that will create for population around the world is unimaginable. Don’t think only poor people will die they will kill the rich and rich will die. Why is soil going away, where is it going away ? See how will sand become soil it will become when we add organic content to it. How soil become sand when you extract out the organic content from it. If you go in the rainforest the organic content in the soil will be about 70%. In normal agricultural soil the minimum organic content that has to be there to call soil as a soil is 3-6 percent, 3% is the most minimum. But today for example in India 62% of India's soils the organic content is 0.5% or below that. So literally we’re going to verge of desertification, this is across the world this is not in one country. In United States the way the land has been used in terms of herbicides and weedicides pesticides. Well we need to understand nearly 82-86% of the biodiversity is within the first 12 inches of that top soil. When we plough with machines like tractors, plough to the depth of 9-12 inches and leave it open, this means which we are destroying the biodiversity completely . What biodiversity means is it is the humus in the soil. The word human come from the word humus, that means the human is actually living soil. Land is getting hot simply because it is ploughed or it is paved. Soil is very significant carbon sink and soil is the largest water soak on the planet and the soil is all the basis of life. To keep the biodiversity alive is the most important thing that we need to do right now.
